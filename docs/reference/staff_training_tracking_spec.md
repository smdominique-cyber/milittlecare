# Staff Training Tracking — Verified Regulatory Requirements

**Source:** Michigan Administrative Code R 400.1901–1963, *Licensing Family and Group Child Care Homes*, adopted by the Department of Lifelong Education, Advancement, and Potential (MiLEAP), Child Care Licensing Bureau. Effective April 27, 2026.

**Scope:** Requirements relevant to PR #8 (staff training tracking for licensed family and group child care homes). One row per verifiable requirement, quoted directly from the adopted rule text.

| Requirement | Citation | Rule text |
|---|---|---|
| Child care assistant must be 14 to 15 years of age | R 400.1921(1) | "A child care assistant shall be 14 to 15 years of age." |
| Child care assistant must be determined eligible before contact with children | R 400.1921(2) | "Before contact with children, the individual shall be determined by the department to be eligible to serve as a child care assistant, as required by R 400.1903(1)(r) and R 400.1903(1)(s)." |
| Child care assistant must provide CPR certification within 90 days of hire | R 400.1921(3)(a) | "Within 90 days of hire, a child care assistant shall provide the licensee both of the following: (a) A valid certification in pediatric, infant, child, and adult CPR." |
| Child care assistant must provide pediatric first aid certification within 90 days of hire | R 400.1921(3)(b) | "Within 90 days of hire, a child care assistant shall provide the licensee both of the following: … (b) A valid certification in pediatric first aid." |
| Child care assistant must be directly supervised at all times | R 400.1921(4) | "A child care assistant shall be supervised directly at all times by the licensee or a child care staff member who is present on-site and can observe the child care assistant." |
| Child care assistant cannot substitute for licensee or staff member | R 400.1921(6) | "A child care assistant shall not substitute for the licensee or a child care staff member." |
| Child care assistant cannot drive vehicle transporting children | R 400.1921(7) | "A child care assistant shall not drive a vehicle that is transporting children." |
| Child care staff member must be 16 years of age or older | R 400.1920(1) | "An individual who is employed as a child care staff member in a child care home shall be 16 years of age or older, pursuant to section 1 of the act, MCL 722.111." |
| Child care staff member must be determined eligible before contact with children | R 400.1920(2) | "Before contact with children, the individual shall be determined by the department to be eligible to serve as a child care staff member, pursuant to sections 5n and 5q of the act, MCL 722.115n and 722.115q, and as required by R 400.1919." |
| Child care staff member must have CPR certification before caring for children | R 400.1920(3)(a) | "Before caring for children at a child care home, an individual shall provide the licensee both of the following: (a) A valid certification in pediatric, infant, child, and adult CPR." |
| Child care staff member must have pediatric first aid certification before caring for children | R 400.1920(3)(b) | "Before caring for children at a child care home, an individual shall provide the licensee both of the following: … (b) A valid certification in pediatric first aid." |
| Applicant/licensee must hold CPR and pediatric first aid certification | R 400.1902(1)(d) | "Have proof of certification in pediatric first aid and pediatric, infant, child, and adult cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) training." |
| Applicant/licensee must have documented training in recognition and reporting of child abuse and neglect | R 400.1902(1)(e)(i) | "Have documentation of completed training in both of the following: (i) Recognition and reporting of child abuse and neglect." |
| Applicant/licensee must have documented training in prevention and control of infectious disease, including immunizations | R 400.1902(1)(e)(ii) | "Have documentation of completed training in both of the following: … (ii) Prevention and control of infectious disease, including immunizations." |
| All staff must complete new hire training within 90 days after being present and before unsupervised care | R 400.1923(1) | "All staff shall participate in the child care home's new hire training within 90 days after being present at the child care home and before caring for children unsupervised." |
| New hire training must cover prevention of shaken baby syndrome | R 400.1923(2)(a) | "The child care home's new hire training must include all of the following areas: (a) The prevention of shaken baby syndrome." |
| New hire training must cover SIDS prevention and safe sleep practices | R 400.1923(2)(b) | "(b) The prevention of sudden infant death syndrome and use of safe sleep practices." |
| New hire training must cover prevention of abusive head trauma | R 400.1923(2)(c) | "(c) The prevention of abusive head trauma." |
| New hire training must cover prevention of child maltreatment | R 400.1923(2)(d) | "(d) The prevention of child maltreatment." |
| New hire training must cover recognition and reporting of child abuse and neglect | R 400.1923(2)(e) | "(e) The recognition and reporting of child abuse and neglect." |
| New hire training must cover prevention and control of infectious diseases, including immunizations | R 400.1923(2)(f) | "(f) The prevention and control of infectious diseases, including immunizations." |
| New hire training must cover emergency preparedness and response planning | R 400.1923(2)(g) | "(g) Emergency preparedness and response planning as described in R 400.1939." |
| New hire training must cover administration of medication | R 400.1923(2)(h) | "(h) Administration of medication." |
| New hire training must cover prevention of and response to food/allergic reaction emergencies | R 400.1923(2)(i) | "(i) Prevention of and response to emergencies due to food and allergic reactions as provided in R 400.1937." |
| New hire training must cover building and physical premises safety | R 400.1923(2)(j) | "(j) Building and physical premises safety, including identification of and protection from hazards, bodies of water, and vehicular traffic." |
| New hire training must cover hazardous materials handling and biocontaminant disposal | R 400.1923(2)(k) | "(k) Handling and storage of hazardous materials and appropriate disposal of biocontaminants." |
| New hire training must cover transportation precautions if applicable | R 400.1923(2)(l) | "(l) Precautions in transporting children if the child care home provides transportation with children at any time." |
| New hire training must cover child development across major domains | R 400.1923(2)(m) | "(m) Child development, including the major domains of cognitive, social, emotional, physical development, and approaches to learning." |
| New hire training must cover pediatric first aid and CPR unless valid certification already presented | R 400.1923(2)(n) | "(n) Pediatric first aid and pediatric, infant, child, and adult CPR, unless the new staff presents a valid certification pursuant to R 400.1920(3)(a), R 400.1920(3)(b), R 400.1921(3)(a), and 400.1921(3)(b)." |
| Staff must be supervised until new hire training is completed | R 400.1923(3) | "Until a staff member has completed the new hire training, the staff is supervised by the licensee or child care staff member who has completed the new hire training." |
| New hire training verification on file at child care home for 2 years after effective date; thereafter via MiRegistry | R 400.1923(4) | "Verification of all training required by this rule must be maintained on file at the child care home until 2 years after the effective date of this rule, after which qualifications must be reflected as verified in MiRegistry." |
| Licensee must complete at least 10 clock hours of professional development each calendar year | R 400.1924(1) | "A licensee shall complete not less than 10 clock hours of professional development each calendar year related to any of the following: (a) Child development. (b) Caring for children. (c) Program planning. (d) Administrative management for a child care business. (e) Updates to topics covered in R 400.1923." |
| Personnel (other than licensee) must complete at least 5 clock hours of professional development each calendar year | R 400.1924(2) | "Personnel, not including the licensee, shall complete not less than 5 clock hours of professional development each calendar year related to any of the following: (a) Child development. (b) Caring for children. (c) Updates to topics covered in R 400.1923." |
| Unsupervised volunteer must complete at least 1 hour of professional development each calendar year | R 400.1924(3) | "An unsupervised volunteer shall complete not less than 1 hour of professional development each calendar year related to all of the following: (a) Child development. (b) Caring for children. (c) Updates to topics covered in R 400.1923." |
| Driver must complete at least 1 hour of professional development each calendar year | R 400.1924(4) | "A driver shall complete not less than 1 hour of professional development each calendar year related to all of the following: (a) Caring for children. (b) Transportation. (c) Updates to topics covered in R 400.1923." |
| CPR and first aid certification hours may count toward professional development hours in the calendar year taken | R 400.1924(5) | "Staff may count CPR and first aid certification hours toward the professional development hours in the calendar year taken." |
| Professional development verification on file at child care home for 2 years after effective date; thereafter via MiRegistry | R 400.1924(7) | "Verification of all professional development required by this rule must be maintained on file at the child care home until 2 years after the effective date of this rule, after which professional development must be reflected as verified in MiRegistry. Completion of the required training must be verified by a document, signed by the trainer or an authorized individual, which must be maintained on file or available online at MiRegistry." |
| CPR and pediatric first aid certifications must be maintained per certification card expiration dates | R 400.1924(8) | "Pediatric first aid and pediatric, infant, child, and adult CPR certification must be maintained according to the expiration dates on the individual's certification cards." |
| Two years after effective date of rule, all professional development training must be MiRegistry-approved | R 400.1924(10) | "Two years after the effective date of this rule, all training to meet professional development requirements must be approved in MiRegistry." |
| Health/safety update documents or activities must be read/completed within timeframe stated in department notice | R 400.1924(11) | "When the department publishes a notice that a new health and safety update document or a new health and safety update training activity was published on MiRegistry, the licensee shall ensure that all applicable personnel and unsupervised volunteers read and acknowledge the document or complete the activity within the timeframe indicated on the notice." |
| All staff must have a MiRegistry account with current membership status within 30 calendar days of employment | R 400.1922(1) | "Within 30 calendar days after employment, all staff shall have an individual MiRegistry account and have a non-expired MiRegistry membership status that would include materials submitted, received, awaiting print, or current." |
| All staff must have a verified program organization employment entry in MiRegistry within 30 calendar days of employment | R 400.1922(2) | "Within 30 calendar days after employment, all staff shall have a verified program organization employment entry in MiRegistry." |
| Staff qualifications maintained on file at child care home for 2 years after effective date; thereafter verified in MiRegistry | R 400.1922(3) | "Verification of the qualifications of all staff must be maintained on file at the child care home 2 years after effective date of this rule, after which qualifications must be verified in MiRegistry." |
| Child care assistants and supervised volunteers cannot administer medication | R 400.1931(1) | "Medication, prescription or nonprescription, must be given to a child in care by a licensee or a child care staff member only. A child care assistant or supervised volunteer shall not give medication to a child in care." |
| Licensee must obtain public sex offender registry clearance for supervised volunteers and child care assistants before contact with children | R 400.1903(1)(r) | "Ensure that all supervised volunteers and child care assistants receive a public sex offender registry clearance by the licensee against the state sex offender registry or the national sex offender registry before having any contact with a child in care. A copy of this clearance must be maintained on file at the child care home." |
| Licensee must have a written policy on screening and supervision of personnel and volunteers, including a sex-offender-registry prohibition | R 400.1903(1)(s) | "Have a written policy regarding screening and supervision of personnel and volunteers, including volunteers who are parents of a child in care. The policy must include a statement that any personnel or volunteer registered on either the state sex offender registry or the national sex offender registry is prohibited from having contact with any child in care." |
| Comprehensive background check eligibility required before unsupervised contact with children for licensee, adult household member, child care staff member, unsupervised volunteer | R 400.1919(1) | "Pursuant to sections 5n and 5q of the act, MCL 722.115n and 722.115q, before an individual having any unsupervised contact with children, the department shall determine the individual's eligibility to be any of the following: (a) A licensee. (b) An adult member of the household. (c) A child care staff member. (d) An unsupervised volunteer." |
| Staff and driver file must include name, address, phone, date of hire, daily arrival/departure records, physician attestation, signed abuse/neglect/discipline acknowledgement | R 400.1906(1) | "A licensee shall maintain a file for each staff and driver that includes all of the following: (a) The individual's first and last name, address, telephone number, and date of hire. (b) Daily records detailing arrival times and departure times at the child care home. (c) A statement signed by a licensed physician or the physician's designee that attests to the individual's mental and physical health … (e) A written statement, signed and dated by the child care staff member or child care assistant at the time of hiring, indicating all of the following: (i) The individual is aware that abuse and neglect of children is unlawful. (ii) The individual knows that they are mandated by law to report child abuse and neglect. (iii) The individual has received and read a copy of the licensee's discipline policy." |
| Staff records retained for duration of employment plus minimum 2 years after termination | R 400.1906(2) | "The records in this rule must be retained for the duration of the individual's employment and a minimum of 2 years after the individual has left the employment of the licensee." |
| Daily attendance records of children must be kept on file at the home with each child's first/last name and arrival/departure times | R 400.1907(3) | "A child care home shall keep on file at the child care home an accurate record of daily attendance at the child care home that includes each child's first and last name and each child's arrival and departure time." |
